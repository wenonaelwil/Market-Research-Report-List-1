<p><p>The high-temperature fibers market comprises three main types: aramid fibers, ceramic fibers, and other specialized fibers. Aramid fibers, known for their excellent strength-to-weight ratio and heat resistance, are widely used in aerospace and military applications. Ceramic fibers, characterized by their thermal stability and low thermal conductivity, are utilized in industrial applications requiring insulation at extreme temperatures. Other fibers may include PBO and carbon fibers, each offering unique properties suited for specific high-temperature environments in industries like automotive, aerospace, and construction.</p></p>
