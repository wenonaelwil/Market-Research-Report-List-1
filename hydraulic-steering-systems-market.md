<p><p>Hydraulic steering systems are crucial for vehicle maneuverability, primarily categorized into mechanical and electronic types. Mechanical hydraulic steering relies on traditional components like pumps and linkages, offering direct feedback and simplicity, making it cost-effective for many applications. In contrast, electronic hydraulic steering employs sensors and actuators to provide enhanced control and adaptability, improving efficiency and response times. The electronic type allows for features like variable assist and integration with advanced driver-assistance systems, driving its growing popularity in modern vehicles.</p></p>
